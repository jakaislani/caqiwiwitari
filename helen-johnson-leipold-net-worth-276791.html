Helen Johnson-Leipold, a prominent businessperson based in the United States, is estimated to have a net worth of $3.57 billion in 2024. As the scion of the Johnson family, which founded the multinational household products company SC Johnson, she has played a crucial role in the growth and success of the business. Helen Johnson-Leipold has held various leadership positions within the company, including serving as the Chairwoman and CEO of Johnson Outdoors, a subsidiary of SC Johnson. With her extensive experience and expertise in the business world, she has made significant contributions to both the family business and the wider industry.

She graduated from Cornell University, as did her parents and siblings, and currently runs Johnson Financial, a financial services company, and Johnson Outdoors, a camping and military supply company. She and her brother, H. Fisk Johnson, the current CEO of SC Johnson and Co., serve as the liaisons to the public for the company. In addition to her work with Johnson Financial and Johnson Outdoors, she is also the chair of the Johnson Foundation at Wingspread, one of the many buildings designed by Frank Lloyd-Wright that the Johnson family oversees. She briefly chaired her brother, S. Curtis Johnson's subsidiary company, Diversey, Inc., after he stepped down due to charges of repeated sexual contact with a child. The Johnson family subsequently sold the subsidiary.
