Sondra Locke (born May 28, 1944 or 1947) is an American actress, singer, and film director who has worked in a variety of genres. When she appeared in the film The Heart Is a Lonely Hunter in 1968, she was nominated to get an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actress. Later in her career, she appeared in films such as Willard (1971), and six films with then-partner Clint Eastwood (1976–1983), including The Outlaw Josey Wales, The Gauntlet, Every Which Way But Loose, Bronco Billy, Any Which Way You Can, and Sudden Impact. ‘The Good, the Bad, and the Very Ugly – A Hollywood Journey’ was the title of her autobiography, which was released in 1997.
